Professional Electrical Services for Chicago-Area Homes

Electrical work is not something you want to leave to guesswork. Faulty wiring, overloaded circuits, and improperly installed outlets are among the leading causes of residential fires in the United States. At ECV Handyman Services, owner Ricky Vargas brings more than 15 years of hands-on electrical experience to every job, ensuring that your home's electrical system is safe, reliable, and up to current Illinois building codes. We serve homeowners throughout Chicago, Oak Park, Cicero, Berwyn, Forest Park, Maywood, River Forest, and Melrose Park.

Electrical Services We Provide

From simple outlet swaps to complex wiring projects, we handle a full range of residential electrical work. Here is what you can count on us for:

Outlet Installation & Repair

Outdated two-prong outlets, dead outlets, and outlets that spark when you plug something in are all safety concerns that should be addressed promptly. We install standard outlets, GFCI outlets for kitchens and bathrooms, USB-equipped outlets for convenient device charging, and dedicated 20-amp outlets for high-draw appliances like window air conditioners and space heaters. Many older homes in the Chicago area, especially bungalows and two-flats built before the 1960s, still have ungrounded outlets that we can upgrade to meet modern safety standards.

Light Fixture Installation

Whether you are hanging a new pendant light over your kitchen island, replacing a dated flush-mount ceiling fixture, or installing recessed lighting throughout your living space, we handle the wiring, mounting, and testing so everything works perfectly. We also install under-cabinet lighting, vanity lights, outdoor security lights, and landscape lighting. If you have purchased a fixture online or from a local store and need it professionally installed, that is one of our most common calls.

Ceiling Fan Installation

Ceiling fans are a smart investment for Chicago homeowners. They circulate warm air downward during the winter months, reducing heating costs, and provide cooling airflow in the summer. Proper installation requires a fan-rated electrical box capable of supporting the weight and vibration of the fan. We install ceiling fans in rooms that already have a light fixture, and we can also run new wiring to add a ceiling fan where none existed before, including the addition of wall switches or remote controls.

Switch Replacement & Installation

We replace worn-out or faulty switches, install dimmer switches for adjustable room lighting, add three-way switches so you can control lights from two locations, and install smart switches compatible with home automation systems. If you have ever walked into a room and flipped a switch that does nothing, we can trace the circuit, identify the issue, and get it working the way it should.

Wiring & Rewiring

Older Chicago homes, particularly those built before 1950, may still have knob-and-tube wiring or early Romex that is deteriorating with age. These systems were designed for a fraction of the electrical load that modern households demand. We perform partial and whole-house rewiring, running new circuits to support today's appliances, home offices, and entertainment systems. We also install dedicated circuits for electric dryers, ranges, and EV chargers, an increasingly common request across the western suburbs.

Electrical Panel Upgrades

If your home still has a 60-amp or 100-amp panel and you are experiencing tripped breakers regularly, it may be time for an upgrade. We install 200-amp panels that can handle the full electrical demands of a modern household, including central air conditioning, electric cooking, and home office equipment. We also replace outdated fuse boxes with modern circuit breaker panels, improving both safety and convenience. Panel upgrades are especially important in areas like Oak Park and River Forest, where beautifully maintained older homes often have electrical systems that have not kept pace with the times.

Circuit Breaker Repair & Replacement

A breaker that trips repeatedly is telling you something. It could be an overloaded circuit, a short circuit, or a ground fault. We diagnose the root cause, replace faulty breakers, and redistribute loads across your panel when needed. We also install AFCI breakers, which are now required by the National Electrical Code in most living areas and provide critical protection against arc faults that can cause fires.
