Chicago Weather and Your Home

If you have lived through even one Chicago winter, you know what the weather can do to a house. Temperatures that plunge well below zero, heavy snow loads on roofs, ice dams forming along gutters, and freeze-thaw cycles that crack masonry and buckle sidewalks. Then summer arrives with its own set of problems: high humidity that warps wood, promotes mold growth, and causes paint to peel.

At ECV Handyman Services, we help Chicago homeowners stay ahead of weather-related damage. Before winter, we seal windows and doors, insulate exposed pipes to prevent freezing, check weatherstripping, and clear gutters to reduce ice dam risk. After winter, we repair the damage that cold weather leaves behind: cracked caulk around windows, damaged exterior trim, moisture-stained ceilings from ice dam leaks, and radiator issues. We also offer 24/7 emergency service for urgent problems like burst pipes, electrical failures, and storm damage, because Chicago weather does not wait for business hours.

Common Repair Needs for Chicago Homes

Plaster walls and ceilings are found in the vast majority of Chicago homes built before 1960. Settlement cracks, water damage, and aging can cause plaster to crack, bulge, or fall away from the wood lath beneath. We repair plaster properly, matching the original texture rather than simply covering problems with drywall.

Aging plumbing systems in older Chicago homes often feature galvanized steel supply lines and cast-iron drain stacks. These materials corrode over decades, leading to low water pressure, slow drains, and eventual leaks. We can replace sections, clear blockages, and advise on full replumbing when necessary.

Knob-and-tube wiring is still present in many Chicago homes built before 1940. While not inherently dangerous if undisturbed, it cannot safely support modern electrical loads. We upgrade outlets, add circuits, and work carefully around existing wiring to bring homes up to current code.

Radiator and boiler maintenance is critical in a city where steam and hot-water radiator systems heat thousands of homes. We bleed radiators, replace valves, address banging pipes, and ensure heating systems are ready before the first cold snap.
